    What is an escrow service

    Escrow is a defined process whereby a buyer puts money into the custody of a trusted third party with a guarantee that funds are available for payment to the seller after a specified set of conditions has been met.

    How does an escrow service work

    It's very simple The Buyer deposits funds into the escrow's custody. The Seller ships item or performs work. The Buyer approves. The escrow service pays the Seller. It's that easy.

    Why should we call upon your escrow services when many sites offer their own escrow

    Bear in mind that if you come across a site who offers their own escrow services, then by definition, escrow is no longer impartial. In such a situation, favoritism, unfair practices, disputes are more likely to occur. Let us consider a situation where a dispute arises: One user is a long-time client who has brought a lot of business and another user who is a new client. If in-house escrow handles such a transaction but a dispute surfaces, chances are in-house escrow will favor their long-time client. To remain true to our code of ethics and to our customers, escrow must remain unbiased. Our services are impartial. As a third party, we do not take sides. For instance, the same code of ethics applies to self-escrow.
